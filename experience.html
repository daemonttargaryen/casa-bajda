Every Morning

Breakfast, the Maltese way.

We don't serve continental breakfasts in plastic wrappers. Every morning between 7:30 and 9:30, we lay out a full Maltese spread on the terrace (weather permitting) or in the dining room.

Everything is made or sourced locally. The bread comes from the bakery two streets away. The cheeselets are from a farm in Rabat. The eggs are from our neighbour's chickens.

Local Tips

Things only locals know.

Fifteen years of hosting guests means we know exactly what makes a trip to Malta memorable. Here are a few of our best-kept secrets.

1
St. Peter's Pool on a weekday
Malta's most beautiful natural swimming pool is 10 minutes away. Go before 9am or after 5pm in summer to have it nearly to yourself.
2
Marsaxlokk Sunday market
The best fish market in Malta, 10 minutes away. Go early for the freshest catch and stay for a plate of grilled lampuki at the waterfront.
3
Mdina at dusk
Malta's ancient walled capital is magical after the day-trippers leave. Take the bus at 5pm and have the streets almost to yourself.
4
Dingli Cliffs for sunset
The highest point in Malta with unobstructed views over the Mediterranean. Best reached by car; we can tell you exactly where to park.
5
The best pastizzi in Malta
Crystal Palace in Rabat. Open 24 hours. Order the ricotta ones. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.
6
Avoid the Valletta crowds
Go Tuesday–Thursday. Skip the Three Cities boat trip (overrated). Walk the Upper Barrakka at 8am — the views are the same and you'll have them alone.
